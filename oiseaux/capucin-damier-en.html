Checkerboard Capuchin

The sexes are identical. Whole head, face and nape are dark reddish or chestnut brown. the mantle, back and scapulars are browner with faint pale buff streaks. The rump is light buff-brown barred and flecked with dark brown. The upperwing-coverts show a nice tint yellowish or straw-yellow. The tail, short and wedge-shaped, has pointed central rectrices. The basis is hidden by the long upperwing-coverts but the visible terminal part is dark brown in the center and yellowish or golden brown on outer tail feathers. The medium and large wing coverts, likewise that the scapulars, the aloula and the primaries display a dark brown-chestnut coloration.

Size: 12cm
Weight: 12 to 15g

Order: Passeriformes
Family: Estrildidae
Genre: Lonchura
Species: punctulata

Its contact call is a very high-pitched nasal 'tay'. It also produces a chirp very characteristic of capuchins. Its song is an almost inaudible series of five notes.
