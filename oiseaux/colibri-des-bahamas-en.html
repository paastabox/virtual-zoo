Bahamas Hummingbird

The silhouette is remarkable for the fact that the beak is short and almost straight. A small white dot is present at the back of the eye. The back is iridescent green. The tail shows a very deep fork. The chin and the throat are bright violet-purple. The lower flanks are russet, emphasizing the very forked shape of the tail. The central rectrices are green, the other rectrices are partially cinnamon-rufous. After the breeding season, the magenta color of the throat is replaced by an eclipse gray. Females have a dull green upperparts. The pale gray chin and throat have circular spots with small green discs. A whitish band crosses the chest. The belly is red. The lateral rectrices are cinnamon with a black subterminal band. Juveniles closely resemble the female. As with all chicks in this age category, we sees spots and streaks on the body plumage: cinnamon on the throat and magenta on the chest. In young females, the feathers on the underside are buff in color with terminal edges Grey.

Size: 8cm
Weight: 2 to 3 g

Order: Apodiformes
Family: Trochilidae
Genus: Nesophlox
Species: evelynae

The songs of race lyrura are slightly shorter and less sophisticated than those of nominate race. The songs of the nominate race are "prítitidee, prítitidee, prítitidee" which have a sustained rhythm. "chii" or "tih" are uttered regularly at night from a perch.
